Can it be argued that, one positive of the widespread of availability and public usage of Large Language Models (AI) is a  potential cognitive alleviation of certain individuals? Does this modern life, that was  already technology and information heavy, produce a cognitive overhead for humans that adversely affects mental well-being? If so, perhaps the evolution of AI can and has the potential to further help people to make decisions or do things on a device that would have taken much longer before. Obviously this is one of the advertisements of AI, the task helper. Whether that to be to book a trip, code a piece of software, or auto-manage your investments. Also it can help with decision making and planning. People need to bounce ideas off with somebody in order to arrive at sharpen their own ideas. Sometimes AI could even help with new ideas or better configurations, but of course models have shown a tendency to mirror the user in some way.
